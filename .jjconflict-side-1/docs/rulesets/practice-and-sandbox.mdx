title: Practice and Sandbox
description: Reference for permissive Akron rulesets used for room-lab routing, testing, and experimentation.
---

`Practice` and `Sandbox` are Akron rulesets for workflows where learning, routing, testing, or inspection matters more than clean-submission status.

`Practice` and `Sandbox` are ruleset presets, not attempt statuses. The attempt status is still Goldberry/Hardlist clean, Regular clean, or Cheat.

Select these rulesets through Akron's Everest mod options, the `akron_ruleset` command, or imported ruleset data. The Akron overlay shows the current ruleset stack and supports `.akr` import/export, but it is not designed as a ruleset picker.

## Practice

`Practice` is the room-lab workflow. It enables room-review displays by default: stamina, speed, dash, input viewer, room timer, and death stats.

`Practice` is useful when a setup or verification flow needs tools such as StartPos restore, room reloads, frame advance, freeze, timescale, Auto Kill, and trigger viewers. Those state-changing tools remain off until you enable them individually.

Using Cheat-classified tools under `Practice` marks the attempt as Cheat. That is expected behavior for a room-lab ruleset.

## Sandbox

`Sandbox` removes all Akron policy blocks. It does not auto-enable features.

Use `Sandbox` for:

- Local experimentation.
- Testing risky combinations.
- Debugging interactions between tools.
- Trying map or runtime behavior before committing it to a setup.

`Sandbox` should not be used as evidence that a feature is clean.

## Map-maker

`Map-maker` favors inspection, reload, and debug traversal without automatically turning on overt gameplay cheats. It is the ideal ruleset for:

- Map capture and recording.
- Room or entity inspection.
- Hitbox visualization.
- Camera or zoom inspection tools.
- Preparing map-specific `.akr` packs.

## Best Practices

To maintain attempt integrity and organizational clarity:

- Keep clean-run setups small and conservative.
- Keep `Practice` and `Sandbox` setups explicit.
- Export scoped packs when sharing map setups.
- Do not import whole-sandbox setups into clean-run contexts.
