title: Visual and accessibility
description: Tune visual noise, audio presentation, HUD visibility, screenshake, and Madeline visuals.
---

Akron includes visual and audio settings for comfort, clarity, and presentation. These settings are usually `Goldberry/Hardlist clear` or `Normal clear` when they do not mutate gameplay or reveal hidden state, but strict submissions may still evaluate presentation changes case by case.

## Visual Noise And Presentation

| Area | Examples |
|---|---|
| Reduced visual noise | No particles, no trails, no glitch, no anxiety, no distortion, hidden weather or effects. |
| Visual tuning | Lighting, bloom, tint, and related presentation values. |
| HUD visibility | Hide or reduce HUD surfaces. |
| Pause menu visibility | Hide the pause menu surface without changing pause state. |
| Death visuals | Suppress post-death particles and wipe presentation. |
| Respawn animation | Shorten post-death respawn presentation. |

## Audio Presentation

Akron can lower volume beyond normal constraints, split audio routes, change active audio playback speed, or pitch-shift audio. These are presentation/accessibility settings in Akron's current policy when they do not change simulation timing.

<Note>
  Audio speed changes active audio playback speed. It does not change the game simulation tick rate.
</Note>

## Madeline Visuals

Akron supports local visual customization such as custom trails, Madeline hair length, and effect sync with active hair color. These change presentation without moving the player or changing collision.

## Screenshake

Screenshake reduction is Goldberry/Hardlist clear because it is an accessibility comfort control that suppresses camera shake without changing gameplay state.

## Configuration Advice

- Keep accessibility and presentation settings in a dedicated accessibility setup when they are your default way to play.
- Keep camera inspection tools such as free camera, camera offset, and cursor zoom in explicit map-inspection or testing setups.
- Do not assume a `Goldberry/Hardlist clear` or `Normal clear` presentation feature is accepted by every strict external rule source.
- Use [Rulesets and status](/concepts/rulesets-and-status) when preparing for a submitted run.
